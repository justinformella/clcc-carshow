# Future Race Tracks

Tracks to add after the initial 3 (Lakefront Sprint, Downtown Dash, Route 14 Blitz) ship.

## Track 4: Three Oaks Run

- **Route:** Teckler Blvd → south to Rakow Road → east past Three Oaks Recreation Area → Pingree Road → loop back via Route 14
- **Vibe:** Open terrain, quarry lakes glinting turquoise, wetlands, prairie grass. Dawn palette — mist over the water, long shadows, birds. The most "open road" feeling route.
- **Hazards:** Gravel shoulders, wildlife crossings, fog patches that reduce visibility
- **Character:** The scenic track. Open landscape, water features, nature all around.
- **Real landmarks:** Three Oaks Recreation Area (reclaimed quarry), turquoise quarry lakes, scuba diving zone, wetlands, Rakow Road, Pingree Road

## Track 5: Hillside Scenic

- **Route:** Teckler Blvd → south through back roads → Hillside Avenue (designated McHenry County scenic drive, 3.5 miles) → rolling hills, oak groves → Terra Cotta Road
- **Vibe:** Winding country road through ancient oak trees, farmland, rolling elevation changes. The "rally stage" — closest to a European countryside sprint. Autumn palette — red/orange canopy, falling leaves.
- **Hazards:** Elevation changes (blind crests), tight curves through trees, fallen leaves (reduced grip)
- **Character:** The rally track. Winding, hilly, the most demanding driving.
- **Real landmarks:** Hillside Avenue scenic drive, Terra Cotta Road (named for historic American Terra Cotta & Ceramic Co.), oak groves, McHenry County farmland

## Other Potential Tracks

- **Veteran Acres Trail** — Through the wooded park trails, past the Nature Center and boardwalk. A tight, technical off-road style track.
- **Lippold Park Loop** — 310-acre mega-park with 5+ miles of trails, ponds, wetlands. A long endurance-style route.
- **Randall Road Gauntlet** — North-south arterial carrying 28,000-39,000 vehicles/day. Pure traffic-dodging chaos.
