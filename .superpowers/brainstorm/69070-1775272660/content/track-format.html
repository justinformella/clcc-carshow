What's the race format?

How does a race play out — pick one

A — Circuit Laps

Race 3-5 laps around a closed track. Multiple turns, overtaking opportunities, pit strategy optional. Classic motorsport feel — Daytona, F1, NASCAR. Tracks can be themed (city streets, country roads, night circuit).

B — Point-to-Point Sprint

Start line to finish line, no laps. The track scrolls as you drive — winding roads, elevation changes, obstacles. Like an OutRun stage or a hill climb. Each track is a unique route. Simpler to build — no loop logic needed.

C — Battle Race (Mario Kart Style)

Circuit racing PLUS items and power-ups. Pick up crates for boosts, oil slicks, EMPs. Adds chaos and comeback mechanics. Most fun multiplayer potential, most complex to balance.

My take

Start with A (circuit laps) — it's the foundation. Once laps work, adding items (C) is just a layer on top. B is a different game entirely. Build A, then layer C on later if you want the Mario Kart chaos.
