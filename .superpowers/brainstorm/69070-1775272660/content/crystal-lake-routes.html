Crystal Lake Race Routes

All routes start from Redline Motor Condos (Teckler Blvd). Based on real Crystal Lake geography. Pick your favorites (multi-select).

1

Lakefront Sprint

Route: Teckler Blvd → Route 14 → Crystal Lake Ave → Dole Ave → Lakeshore Drive → finish at Main Beach

Vibe: Starts industrial, transitions through tree-lined residential streets, ends at the lake with water reflections and the Dole Mansion in the background. Sunset palette — golden hour over the water.

Hazards: Sharp turn onto Dole Ave, narrow lakeside road, pedestrians near the beach

The signature track. Short, scenic, emotional finish at the water.

2

Downtown Dash

Route: Teckler Blvd → Route 14 → Williams Street → Crystal Lake Ave → past the Raue Center → through the historic downtown grid → Virginia Street

Vibe: Tight small-town streets, brick storefronts, the 1929 Raue Center theater marquee glowing, café tables on sidewalks, street lamps. Night race with neon signs and shop windows.

Hazards: Tight corners through the grid, parked cars on Williams St, crosswalks

The technical track. Tight turns, small-town charm, lots of pixel art detail.

3

Route 14 Blitz

Route: Teckler Blvd → east on Route 14 (Northwest Highway) → past strip malls, Best Buy, shopping centers → through the commercial corridor → finish near Randall Road

Vibe: Wide-open 4-lane highway, neon signs, parking lots, fast food joints. High speed, long straights. Late-night palette — dark sky, lit signs, headlight glow on asphalt.

Hazards: Traffic lights (run them or lose time), cross traffic at intersections, delivery trucks

The speed track. Wide lanes, high speed, dodge traffic. American commercial strip at night.

4

Three Oaks Run

Route: Teckler Blvd → south to Rakow Road → east past Three Oaks Recreation Area → Pingree Road → loop back via Route 14

Vibe: Open terrain, quarry lakes glinting turquoise, wetlands, prairie grass. Dawn palette — mist over the water, long shadows, birds. The most "open road" feeling route.

Hazards: Gravel shoulders, wildlife crossings, fog patches that reduce visibility

The scenic track. Open landscape, water features, nature all around.

5

Hillside Scenic

Route: Teckler Blvd → south through back roads → Hillside Avenue (designated McHenry County scenic drive) → rolling hills, oak groves → Terra Cotta Road

Vibe: Winding country road through ancient oak trees, farmland, rolling elevation changes. The "rally stage" — closest to a European countryside sprint. Autumn palette — red/orange canopy, falling leaves.

Hazards: Elevation changes (blind crests), tight curves through trees, fallen leaves (reduced grip)

The rally track. Winding, hilly, the most demanding driving.

My recommendation

Start with 3 tracks for launch. I'd pick 1 (Lakefront) as the intro/signature, 2 (Downtown) for technical variety, and 3 (Route 14) for pure speed. Add 4 and 5 later — they're great but less essential to start.
